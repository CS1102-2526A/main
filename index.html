What is Acid-base reaction?

Acid–base reactions are one of the most important processes in the chemistry world. It is known that Svante Arrhenius is the first person to clearly explain this idea. Acid–base reactions will occur when an acid provides protons (H⁺ ions)and a base accepts them. Through the processes, they often produce water and salt. These reactions are not only fundamental in laboratories but also important in everyday life — from digestion in our stomachs to the neutralization of pollutants in the environment.


What is neutralisation?

When there is an exact amount of both alkali and acid mixed, they will cancel each other out and form a neutral solution. The reaction is called neutralisation.

Common Examples of neutralization in our daily life

  1. Personal Health & Hygiene
    • Relief from a Stomach Upset: The stomach produces hydrochloric acid for digestion. When your stomach has too much acid, you can make use of antacid tablets, which contain alkalis, to neutralize the extra acid.
    • Prevent Cavity: Do you know that germs produce acid in your mouth, which can damage teeth? It is important to brush your teeth using toothpaste as it contains slightly alkaline ingredients, to help cancel out these harmful acids and keep your teeth strong.

  2. Household Cleaning
    • Eliminate Limescale: Limescale is an alkaline ingredient. You can make use of cleaning products with weak acids, such as vinegar, to dissolve the stains through the neutralization reaction.

What is pH-Value?

pH is a scale that measures how acidic or basic a water-based solution is. It ranges from 0 to 14 and is a simple method to show how many hydrogen ions(H⁺) in a solution.

Acidic Solutions (pH below 7): They contain more H⁺ ions. The higher the acidity, the lower the pH.
Examples: Lemon juice and vinegar

Neutral Solutions (pH of 7): They have the same amount of H⁺ and hydroxide (OH⁻) ions.
Example: Clean water

Basic Solutions (pH above 7): They have more H⁺ ions and fewer level of OH⁻ ions. Therefore, The more the basicity, the higher the pH.
Example: Baking soda and soap
